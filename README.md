Project Overview:-
The primary objective of this analysis was to evaluate iPhone sales performance in the Indian market, focusing on pricing strategies, customer satisfaction (ratings), and the impact of discounts.

Key Data Insights
Pricing Structure: The dataset reveals a wide price range, with sale prices spanning from ₹29,999 to ₹140,900. The average sale price across the 62 models analyzed is approximately ₹80,073.

Customer Engagement: Products in the dataset have a high level of engagement, with an average of 22,420 ratings per model. The highest-rated models achieved a star rating of 4.7 out of 5.

Discounting Trends: Apple products maintained an average discount of nearly 10%, though some models featured aggressive discounts of up to 29% to drive volume.

Visual Analysis & Performance
Top Rated Models: You identified the highest-rated iPhones, prominently featuring the iPhone 11 Pro Max (Midnight Green, 64 GB) and the iPhone 8 (Gold, 256 GB) as top performers in customer satisfaction.

Rating Distribution: A bar chart was utilized to visualize the "Number of Ratings" across different models, which helps identify which versions (like the iPhone SE and iPhone 11 series) have the highest market penetration and consumer feedback volume.

Price vs. Discount Relationship: You developed a bubble graph to examine the relationship between discount percentages and the number of ratings, using a trendline to assess how price reductions correlate with consumer response.

Methodology Summary:-
The analysis followed a structured data science workflow:

Objective Setting: Focused on iPhone sales trends.

Data Cleaning: Utilized Pandas and NumPy to ensure data integrity and check for null values.

Descriptive Analysis: Calculated key metrics such as mean, min, and max prices.

Data Visualization: Leveraged the Plotly library to create interactive bar and scatter charts for executive-level reporting.
